About the Author

Emmanuel Daniel

Author Entrepreneur Futurist Fintech Power 50

Emmanuel Daniel is an author, entrepreneur and corporate strategist — a global thought leader on the future of finance and its impact on business and society. He was listed as a top 10 global fintech influencer in the Fintech Power 50 list for 2021 and 2022.

In 1996, Emmanuel founded TAB Global, the research, publication and consulting house behind The Asian Banker, Wealth and Society, The Banking Academy and TAB Insights — giving him extensive contact with banking and finance leaders across the world.

Trained as a lawyer with degrees from the National University of Singapore and the University of London, Emmanuel has visited more than 110 countries and divides his time between Singapore, Beijing and New York. He has been featured on BBC, CNBC and Bloomberg.

Key Concepts

Key Ideas in the Book

The essential arguments that make Building the AI Bank required reading for every leader navigating the intersection of AI and financial institutions.

01
Speed as a Governance Crisis
When AI acts faster than humans can review, banking enters a new risk regime. Ethics failures no longer arise from intention — they emerge from velocity. The book argues that banks must build governance infrastructure that operates at machine speed, not board-meeting speed.
02
Judgment Encoded into Code
The central claim of the book: judgment — once the exclusive domain of experienced bankers — is being encoded into models. This is not just automation; it is the transfer of institutional wisdom to algorithms. Whoever designs the model makes the moral choices.
03
The New Role of the Board
CEOs and board members face fiduciary duties in a world where their most important decisions are made by systems they do not fully understand. Daniel provides a new framework for what board oversight, accountability and governance must look like in AI-era institutions.
04
Customers Disappear from the Loop
As AI intermediates the entire banking relationship — from onboarding to credit decisions to product recommendations — the customer becomes the last to know. The book questions what consent, transparency and inclusion mean when the system acts before anyone asks.
05
From Products to Living Agreements
AI transforms financial products from fixed offers into continuously-updating agreements between institution and individual. A credit line, an insurance policy, a deposit account — all become dynamic instruments recalibrated in real time by models learning from your behaviour.
06
Trust as the Ultimate Currency
The banks that win the AI era will not be the ones with the most powerful models. They will be the ones most trusted to deploy power responsibly. Daniel's central conclusion: accountability is the only sustainable competitive advantage in an age of autonomous banking.
07
Don't Build What You Can Rent
Most banks are building AI infrastructure they cannot sustain. The book argues that proprietary AI stacks are a liability for all but a handful of institutions — and that smart banks will define their AI strategy around what they own, what they partner, and what they abandon entirely.
08
The Agentic Leap
The book distinguishes between AI as automation (doing tasks faster) and AI as agency (making decisions autonomously). The agentic leap — from chatbot to agent that can originate, negotiate and close a financial transaction — is the most consequential moment in banking history since ATMs.
09
Geopolitics Shapes the AI Bank
DeepSeek, sovereign AI clouds, and the fragmentation of the global technology stack mean that AI strategy in banking is now inseparable from geopolitics. Which LLM can a regulated bank use? Who owns the training data? These are the new compliance questions.
